# CGWB Groundwater Quality — Andhra Pradesh State
## Central Ground Water Board, South Eastern Regional Office | 2023

This document summarises groundwater quality findings for Andhra Pradesh
from CGWB monitoring data. Used by WaterSentinel for location-specific
diagnosis in Vijayawada, Guntur, Visakhapatnam and other AP cities.

---

## 1. Overview

Andhra Pradesh has diverse geology — Eastern Ghats in the north-east,
Krishna-Godavari alluvial delta in the centre, and Deccan Plateau basalt
in the west. Groundwater quality varies significantly by district and geology.

---

## 2. Vijayawada (Krishna District) — Groundwater Profile

### 2.1 Geology and Water Sources
Vijayawada sits on the Krishna river delta alluvial plain. Borewell depths:
- Shallow alluvial zone: 30–80 feet — high TDS, hardness, bacterial risk
- Deep alluvial: 100–200 feet — better quality but still elevated TDS
- Rocky outcrops (Indrakeeladri hill area): 150–300 feet

### 2.2 Key Issues in Vijayawada

**TDS and Hardness:**
- Alluvial Krishna delta soils have naturally high dissolved minerals
- TDS commonly 600–1200 mg/L in shallow borewells (above BIS 500 mg/L)
- Hardness 300–600 mg/L common (above BIS acceptable of 200 mg/L)
- White deposits on vessels, taps very common in Vijayawada households
- This is the most common water complaint in the city

**Iron:**
- Above BIS limits (0.3 mg/L) in eastern Vijayawada areas near
  Eluru canal and low-lying flood plain areas
- East Vijayawada (Auto Nagar, Ajit Singh Nagar): elevated iron

**Municipal Supply (VWSS):**
- Vijayawada Water Supply and Sewerage (VWSS) sources from Krishna river
- Treatment plant at Prakasam Barrage generally produces BIS-compliant water
- Quality degradation in old city areas due to aging pipeline network

**Nitrate:**
- Above BIS limits (45 mg/L) in areas near agricultural land
  (outskirts of Vijayawada, Ibrahimpatnam, Mylavaram)
- Cause: Intensive rice and sugarcane cultivation using nitrogen fertilisers

## 3. Guntur District

**Fluoride:**
- 15–20% of tested borewells exceed BIS limit of 1.0 mg/L
- Severely affected mandals: Narasaraopet, Macherla, Piduguralla
- Depth correlation: Fluoride increases with depth in granite areas

**Nitrate:**
- High in agricultural zones (cotton, chilli cultivation)
- Ponnur, Tenali, Repalle taluks most affected

**TDS:**
- Coastal Guntur (Nizampatnam, Bapatla) has saltwater intrusion
- TDS 1000–3000 mg/L in coastal borewells less than 50 feet deep

---

## 4. Prakasam District

**Fluoride — Severe:**
- One of the highest fluoride-affected districts in AP
- Kandukur, Ongole, Markapur areas: fluoride 2.0–8.0 mg/L in places
- Skeletal fluorosis cases documented

**Nitrate:**
- Above limits in tobacco and groundnut cultivation areas

---

## 5. Nellore District

**Saltwater Intrusion — Coastal Areas:**
- Borewells within 5km of coast: TDS 1500–5000 mg/L in many locations
- Chlorides above BIS permissible limit (1000 mg/L)
- Taste: strongly salty

**Fluoride:**
- Above limits in inland areas (Gudur, Kavali taluks)

---

## 6. East and West Godavari Districts

**Iron — Most Common Issue:**
- Iron above BIS limits in 40–50% of tested borewells
- Alluvial Godavari plain soils are iron-rich
- Rajahmundry, Kakinada, Amalapuram, Eluru areas most affected
- Yellow/brown water and pipe staining very common
- Water appears clear when drawn but turns yellow on standing
  (iron oxidises on contact with air)

**WaterSentinel relevance:** This delayed yellowing effect is important —
water that looks clear initially but yellows in a bucket = iron contamination.
Users should check water in a white vessel after 10 minutes.

---

## 7. Kurnool and Kadapa Districts

**Fluoride:**
- Above BIS limits in 30–40% of borewells
- Nandyal, Allagadda, Srisailam areas severely affected

**TDS:**
- Above acceptable limits due to hard rock geology

---

## 8. Visakhapatnam District

**Iron:**
- Above limits in Araku valley and tribal areas (laterite soils)

**TDS:**
- Generally acceptable in most urban Visakhapatnam areas
- Coastal areas show chloride elevation

**Industrial Contamination:**
- Areas near Visakhapatnam port and industrial zone:
  possible heavy metal contamination (cadmium, chromium)
- Recommend laboratory testing for residents near industrial areas

---

## 9. AP-Specific Municipal Supply Context

**Krishna Water Supply:**
- Districts in Krishna-Godavari delta benefit from river surface water
  treated at municipal plants
- Treated river water quality is generally better than groundwater
  in these areas

**AP Drinking Water Supply Corporation (APDWSC):**
- State-level body overseeing rural water supply
- Fluoride mitigation plants installed in high-fluoride areas
- Maintenance quality varies significantly by mandal

---

## 10. Seasonal Variation in AP

**Summer (April–June):**
- Krishna and Godavari water levels low
- Groundwater table drops — deeper borewells required
- Contaminant concentrations increase
- Saltwater intrusion worsens in coastal areas

**Monsoon (July–September):**
- Heavy rainfall in Eastern Ghats and Deccan
- Surface flooding contaminates shallow borewells in delta areas
- Microbial contamination peaks in flood-prone areas
- Iron and TDS temporarily diluted

**Post-monsoon recharge (October–November):**
- Best groundwater quality period for chemical parameters
- Microbial risk remains elevated until contamination flushes out
