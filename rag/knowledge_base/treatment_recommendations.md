## Home and Community Water Treatment for Indian Households

## CRITICAL RULE FOR WATERSENTINEL

Match treatment to contaminant type. Wrong filter = wasted money + false
sense of safety. The most common mistake: buying UV filter for iron
contamination. UV kills bacteria but does NOT remove iron.

## 1. Treatment by Contaminant Type

### 1.1 Hydrogen Sulphide (H2S) — Egg Smell

**Treatment options (in order of effectiveness and cost):**

**Option A — Aeration (Free / Very Low Cost)**
- How: Fill a bucket, pour the water back and forth between two buckets
  10–15 times. H2S gas escapes into air.
- OR: Run water through a sprinkler head or shower nozzle into a bucket.
- Effectiveness: Removes 70–90% of H2S. Safe for drinking after aeration.
- Cost: Free (using existing vessels) to ₹500 (simple aerator fitting)
- Best for: Borewell users who want immediate low-cost solution

**Option B — Activated Carbon Filter**
- How: Inline carbon block filter on tap or under-counter unit.
- Effectiveness: Removes H2S odour effectively. Does not address iron.
- Cost: ₹3,000–8,000 for under-counter unit. Cartridge replacement
  every 6 months: ₹500–1,000.
- Best for: Households where smell is primary complaint

**Option C — Chlorination + Aeration**
- How: Add 0.5mg/L of chlorine (bleaching powder) to storage tank.
  Chlorine oxidises H2S. Aerate to remove oxidised sulphur.
- Effectiveness: Very effective. Also kills bacteria.
- Cost: ₹100–200 per month for bleaching powder
- Best for: Municipal storage tanks with H2S issue

**NOT effective for H2S:**
- UV purifier (kills bacteria, does not remove H2S gas)
- RO alone (may partially reduce H2S but primary purpose is different)
- Iron removal filter (does not address H2S)

### 1.2 Iron (Fe) — Yellow/Brown Water, Orange Staining

**Treatment options:**

**Option A — Iron Removal Filter (Recommended)**
- Technology: Greensand filter or Birm media filter
- How it works: Oxidises dissolved iron (Fe2+) to solid iron (Fe3+)
  which is then filtered out. Backwash periodically to clean.
- Effectiveness: Removes 95%+ of iron. Most effective solution.
- Cost: ₹5,000–15,000 for whole-house filter unit.
  Media replacement every 2–3 years: ₹2,000–4,000.
- Best for: Borewell with iron >0.3 mg/L

**Option B — Oxidation + Settling**
- How: Fill storage tank, add small amount of potassium permanganate
  (pink crystals — available at hardware shops). Wait 30 minutes.
  Iron settles as brown sludge. Draw water from top carefully.
- Effectiveness: Moderate — removes visible iron. Not for daily use.
- Cost: ₹100–200 per month

**Option C — RO System**
- Removes iron along with TDS. If TDS is also a problem, RO addresses both.
- Cost: ₹8,000–25,000. Higher long-term maintenance cost.
- Disadvantage: Wastes 3L of water for every 1L produced.

**NOT effective for iron:**
- UV purifier — kills bacteria, does NOT remove iron
- Activated carbon filter — marginal iron removal, not reliable
- Boiling — does NOT remove iron, may concentrate it slightly

**Important note for WaterSentinel:**
Yellow staining on clothes during washing = iron in water.
White vessels turning yellow = iron. Both are iron indicators.

### 1.3 High TDS / Hard Water — White Deposits, Scale

**Treatment options:**

**Option A — RO (Reverse Osmosis) System (Recommended)**
- How: Water forced through a semi-permeable membrane under pressure.
  Membrane blocks dissolved solids, allows pure water through.
- Effectiveness: Removes 90–96% of TDS. Most effective for TDS.
- Cost: ₹8,000–25,000 for under-sink unit.
  Membrane replacement every 2 years: ₹1,500–3,000.
  Filter replacement every 6 months: ₹800–1,500.
- Best for: TDS above 500 mg/L, kidney stone risk
- Disadvantage: Wastes water (3:1 ratio typical). Removes beneficial
  minerals along with harmful ones. TDS below 100 mg/L is too pure —
  add a mineraliser cartridge.

**Option B — Water Softener (Ion Exchange)**
- How: Replaces calcium and magnesium ions with sodium ions.
  Reduces hardness but does NOT reduce overall TDS.
- Effectiveness: Reduces scale formation but softened water has
  elevated sodium — not recommended for people on low-sodium diet
  or with heart/kidney conditions.
- Cost: ₹8,000–20,000 for whole-house softener.
  Salt (sodium chloride) for regeneration: ₹500/month.

**NOT effective for TDS:**
- Boiling — concentrates TDS by evaporating water, making it WORSE
- UV purifier — kills bacteria, no effect on TDS
- Iron filter — specific to iron, no TDS reduction
- Activated carbon — no TDS reduction

**Kidney stone advisory:**
Residents with history of kidney stones or family history of urological
issues should prioritise RO system. Daily consumption of high-TDS water
(above 500 mg/L) significantly increases kidney stone risk over years.

### 1.4 Bacterial / Coliform Contamination — No Visible Symptom

**Treatment options:**

**Option A — Boiling (Free, Immediate)**
- How: Boil vigorously for at least 1 minute (3 minutes at altitude
  above 2000m, not relevant for Hyderabad/Vijayawada).
- Effectiveness: 99.9% effective against bacteria, viruses, protozoa.
- Cost: Fuel cost only (gas/electricity)
- Best for: Immediate emergency situation, open well users, hand pump users
- Limitation: Does not remove chemical contaminants. Cooled boiled
  water should be stored in clean covered vessel.

**Option B — UV Purifier (Recommended for Long-term)**
- How: UV-C light (wavelength 254nm) destroys bacterial DNA, preventing
  reproduction. Water passes through UV chamber before reaching tap.
- Effectiveness: 99.99% against bacteria and viruses.
- Cost: ₹2,500–6,000 for under-counter unit.
  UV lamp replacement every 8,000 hours (approx. 1 year): ₹800–1,500.
- Limitation: Does NOT remove chemical contaminants (iron, TDS, fluoride).
  Water must be clear — turbid water blocks UV.

**Option C — Chlorination**
- How: Add sodium hypochlorite solution or bleaching powder to storage tank.
  Dose: 2.5ml of 5% bleach per 1000L water.
- Effectiveness: Very effective against bacteria. Less effective against
  some viruses and protozoa (Giardia, Cryptosporidium).
- Cost: ₹100–200 per month
- Best for: Community tanks, hand pump communities

**NOT effective for bacteria:**
- RO alone (membrane has some microbial rejection but not reliable
  without additional disinfection)
- Iron removal filter — no microbial treatment
- Boiling then storing in unclean vessel — re-contamination occurs

### 1.5 Fluoride — No Visible Symptom

**Treatment options:**

**Option A — RO System (Most Practical)**
- Effectiveness: Removes 85–92% of fluoride.
- Cost: ₹8,000–25,000 (same as for TDS)
- Note: If water TDS is also high, RO addresses both.

**Option B — Activated Alumina Filter**
- How: Water passes through alumina beads that adsorb fluoride.
- Effectiveness: Removes 90%+ of fluoride at neutral pH.
- Cost: ₹4,000–10,000. Media replacement every 1–2 years.
- Best for: If fluoride is the only problem and TDS is acceptable.

**NOT effective for fluoride:**
- UV purifier — no effect on chemical contaminants
- Boiling — concentrates fluoride (water evaporates, fluoride stays)
- Carbon filter — no fluoride removal
- Iron removal filter — no fluoride removal

**Critical warning:**
Boiling fluoride-contaminated water makes it MORE concentrated and
MORE dangerous. Never boil as a treatment for fluoride.

---

### 1.6 Manganese — Dark Brown/Black Water

**Treatment:**
- Oxidation filter with manganese greensand or birm media
- OR: Oxidation with potassium permanganate then filtration
- Cost: ₹6,000–18,000 for whole-house filter
- Note: Standard iron removal filters may handle mild manganese.
  For severe manganese, specify manganese-rated filter when purchasing.

---

### 1.7 Nitrate — No Visible Symptom

**Treatment:**
- RO system: Removes 85–92% of nitrates
- Ion exchange: Nitrate-selective resin (specialist product)
- Boiling: Concentrates nitrate — NEVER use for nitrate
- Note: Nitrate is especially dangerous for infants under 6 months.
  If nitrate is suspected and infant is in household, switch to
  bottled water immediately for infant feeding.

## 2. Combined Contamination — Treatment Combinations

Most Indian groundwater has multiple issues. Common combinations:

### 2.1 H2S + Iron (Very Common — BHEL, Nallagandla)
**Recommended:** Aeration unit (removes H2S) + Iron removal filter
**Cost:** ₹8,000–20,000 combined
**Order:** Aerate first, then filter. Aeration oxidises both H2S and Fe2+.

### 2.2 Iron + High TDS (Common — Vijayawada)
**Recommended:** Iron pre-filter + RO system
**Cost:** ₹12,000–30,000 combined
**Order:** Iron filter first (protects RO membrane from iron fouling)

### 2.3 Bacteria + Chemical (Any source type)
**Recommended:** RO + UV combination system
Most under-counter RO systems now include UV as standard (Aquaguard,
Kent, Pureit premium models all offer RO+UV).
**Cost:** ₹12,000–35,000

### 2.4 H2S + Bacteria (Tank contamination)
**Recommended:** Clean storage tank immediately. Add bleaching powder.
Aerate water drawn from tank before drinking.
Long-term: Install aeration + UV combination.

## 3. Water Testing — When and Where

### 3.1 When to Test
- When symptoms don't clearly indicate contaminant
- Before making a major purchase decision (RO system)
- If multiple family members have recurring stomach issues
- If located in high-risk area (Nalgonda for fluoride, coastal for salt)
- After getting a new borewell drilled
- Annually for hand pump water

### 3.2 What to Test For
**Basic test panel (₹500–800):** pH, TDS, turbidity, iron, bacterial
**Comprehensive panel (₹1,500–2,500):** All BIS IS 10500 parameters
**Targeted test:** If specific contaminant suspected (₹200–500 per parameter)

### 3.3 Where to Test in Hyderabad
- **GHMC water testing labs:** Free for municipal supply complaints.
  Submit sample at nearest GHMC office. Results in 3–5 days.
- **HMWSSB testing:** Free for HMWSSB supply issues.
- **Private NABL-accredited labs:**
  - Vimta Labs, Nacharam: Full panel ₹1,500–3,000
  - SGS India, Gachibowli: ₹2,000–4,000
  - CESS Labs: ₹800–2,000
  - Enquire at nearest Apollo or Vijaya diagnostic centre —
    some offer water testing as part of health packages

### 3.4 Where to Test in Vijayawada
- **VMC (Vijayawada Municipal Corporation):** Free testing for VMC
  supply complaints. Contact VMC ward office.
- **AP Pollution Control Board lab, Vijayawada:** Nominal fee.
- **Private labs:** Enquire at Vijayawada District Medical Lab.
