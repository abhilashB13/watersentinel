# Symptom-to-Contaminant Mapping Guide
## Custom Authored for WaterSentinel | Indian Urban/Peri-Urban Context
## Version 1.0 | June 2026

This document is the core diagnostic reference for WaterSentinel's
WaterProfiler agent. It maps observable water quality symptoms to
likely contaminants, with confidence levels, source-type correlations,
and Indian-specific context. All contaminant limits reference BIS IS 10500:2012.

---

## 1. Smell-Based Diagnosis

### 1.1 Egg Smell / Rotten Egg Smell / Sulphur Smell

**Primary contaminant:** Hydrogen Sulphide (H2S)
**Confidence:** Very High (0.95)
**BIS limit:** 0.05 mg/L

**Mechanism:**
H2S is produced by sulphur-reducing bacteria (Desulfovibrio, Desulfotomaculum)
under anaerobic (oxygen-free) conditions. Deep borewells create ideal
anaerobic conditions. H2S can also come from sulphur deposits in certain
rock formations (Deccan Trap basalt).

**Source-specific context:**
- Borewell >200ft: Very high probability of H2S from anaerobic bacteria
- Borewell >300ft in BHEL/Nallagandla/Tellapur: Near certainty of H2S
- Municipal pipeline: Unlikely unless storage tank is contaminated with
  sulphur bacteria (tank not cleaned >12 months)
- Hand pump: Possible if aquifer is deep and anaerobic
- Only from hot water tap: Hot water heater/geyser issue, not source

**Key advisory:**
- SAFE FOR BATHING: H2S at groundwater concentrations is safe for skin
- NOT SAFE FOR DRINKING without treatment
- Does NOT indicate sewage contamination (different smell profile)
- Treatment: Aeration or activated carbon filter

**Distinguishing from sewage smell:**
- H2S: Clean egg smell, appears consistently regardless of how much
  water is run
- Sewage contamination: Faecal/musty smell, may vary, often worse
  after rain or pressure changes

---

### 1.2 Musty / Earthy / Mouldy Smell

**Primary contaminant:** Algae, organic matter, bacterial biofilm
**Confidence:** Medium (0.70)
**Risk level:** Medium — indicates microbial activity

**Source-specific context:**
- Open well: Very common — algae growth in sunlight-exposed water
- Municipal storage tank: Common in tanks not cleaned for >6 months
- Municipal pipeline: Possible in old pipes with biofilm
- Borewell: Unusual — investigate storage tank first

**Advisory:** Possible microbial contamination. Boil water or use UV
purifier. Clean storage tank immediately with bleaching powder.

---

### 1.3 Chlorine Smell

**Not a contamination indicator**
**Confidence:** N/A

Chlorine smell = municipal treatment chemical. Normal and expected in
fresh municipal supply. Indicates HMWSSB/VWSS has chlorinated the water
which is a POSITIVE sign. Chlorine dissipates with time — stored water
may lose chlorine smell while remaining safe.

**Advisory:** Normal. No action required. If very strong smell, let
water stand for 30 minutes before drinking.

---

### 1.4 Petroleum / Diesel / Chemical Smell

**Primary contaminant:** Industrial chemical contamination
**Confidence:** High for chemical contamination (0.85)
**Risk level:** HIGH — do not use for any purpose

**Source-specific context:**
- Borewell near petrol station, factory, or industrial area: High risk
  of petroleum hydrocarbons (BTEX compounds)
- Any source near industrial zone: Possible industrial effluent
  contamination

**Advisory:** STOP USING IMMEDIATELY. Do not drink, bathe, or cook.
Contact CPCB and local municipal authority immediately. Requires
laboratory testing.

---

### 1.5 No Smell

**Does not indicate water is safe**
Many dangerous contaminants are odourless:
- Fluoride: Completely odourless
- Nitrate: Completely odourless
- Arsenic: Completely odourless
- Coliform bacteria: Often odourless
- Lead: Odourless
- High TDS: May be odourless

**Advisory:** Absence of smell does not confirm safety. If reporting
no smell but experiencing stomach issues, recommend laboratory testing
for microbial and chemical parameters.

## 2. Colour-Based Diagnosis

### 2.1 Yellow Water / Light Brown Water

**Primary contaminant:** Iron (Fe)
**Confidence:** Very High (0.90)
**BIS limit:** 0.3 mg/L

**Mechanism:** Ferrous iron (Fe2+) in groundwater is colourless when
first drawn. On contact with oxygen in air, it oxidises to ferric iron
(Fe3+) which is yellow-orange. Water that appears clear when drawn but
turns yellow in a bucket after 10 minutes = iron contamination.

**Source-specific context:**
- Borewell in Deccan Plateau or alluvial plains: Very common
- BHEL, Nallagandla, Godavari delta areas: Near certainty
- Municipal supply that starts clear: Pipeline corrosion or
  distribution network iron

**Secondary contaminant possibility:** Manganese (gives darker brown colour)

**Advisory:**
- Safe for bathing, washing clothes (may stain white fabrics)
- NOT safe for drinking above BIS limit
- Install iron removal filter (greensand/birm media)
- Do NOT buy UV purifier for this problem — UV does not remove iron

---

### 2.2 Dark Brown / Black Water

**Primary contaminants (in order of probability):**
1. Manganese (Mn) — confidence 0.65
2. Sewage contamination — confidence 0.25
3. Degraded pipe material — confidence 0.10

**BIS limit for Mn:** 0.1 mg/L acceptable, 0.3 mg/L permissible

**Distinguishing manganese from sewage:**
- Manganese: No faecal smell, appears consistently, clears somewhat
  on standing
- Sewage: Faecal or musty smell, worse after heavy rain, may contain
  visible particles

**Risk level:** HIGH regardless of cause — do not drink

**Advisory:** STOP DRINKING immediately. If sewage contamination
suspected (smell + black colour), stop all use and contact authorities.
For manganese: oxidation filter + manganese greensand treatment.

---

### 2.3 Blue-Green Water / Blue-Green Staining

**Primary contaminant:** Copper (Cu) from pipe corrosion
**Confidence:** Very High (0.90)
**BIS limit:** 0.05 mg/L acceptable, 1.5 mg/L permissible

**Mechanism:** Acidic water (pH below 7.0) corrodes copper pipes.
Blue-green staining appears on:
- Brass taps and fittings
- White ceramic sinks and bathtubs
- Inside kettles
- White plastic buckets

**Source-specific context:**
- Borewell with low pH + copper pipes: Very high risk
- Old buildings (pre-2000) with copper plumbing: Elevated risk
- New buildings with PVC/CPVC pipes: Very low risk

**Advisory:** Run tap for 30 seconds before collecting drinking water
(flushes standing water that has been in contact with pipes).
Consider replacing copper pipes with CPVC if severe. RO removes copper.

---

### 2.4 Milky White Water That Clears When Standing

**Contaminant:** Dissolved air (not a contaminant)
**Confidence:** Very High (0.92)
**Risk:** None — harmless

**Mechanism:** Air bubbles trapped in water under pressure appear milky.
When water stands, bubbles rise and water clears from bottom up.

**Advisory:** Completely harmless. No action required. Common after
pipe pressure restoration or new connection.

---

### 2.5 Milky White Water That Does NOT Clear

**Primary contaminant:** High turbidity — suspended particles
**Confidence:** High (0.80)
**BIS limit:** 1 NTU acceptable, 5 NTU permissible
**Risk:** Medium-High — possible contamination

**Source-specific context:**
- After heavy rain — borewell or hand pump: Surface runoff contamination
- Municipal supply post-pressure restoration: Pipe disturbance
- Open well: Very common

**Advisory:** Do not drink. Boil after filtering through clean cloth.
If persistent, use ceramic filter or UV purifier. Get water tested.

---

### 2.6 Greenish Water

**Primary contaminants:**
1. Algae growth (open well, exposed tank) — confidence 0.70
2. Copper contamination (see 2.3) — confidence 0.20

**Advisory:** Do not drink. For algae in open well — chlorinate well
with bleaching powder (100g per 1000L water). For copper — see 2.3.

## 3. Taste-Based Diagnosis

### 3.1 Metallic Taste

**Primary contaminants (in order):**
1. Iron (Fe) — very common in Indian groundwater
2. Manganese (Mn) — especially with dark water
3. Copper (Cu) — especially if blue-green staining also present
4. Zinc — from galvanised pipes (older installations)

**Advisory:** Run tap for 30 seconds (flushes pipe-contact water).
Install iron removal filter if iron confirmed. Get water tested.

---

### 3.2 Salty Taste

**Primary contaminants:**
1. High TDS / High chlorides — coastal areas, shallow borewells
2. Sodium — from ion exchange softeners (if over-softened)

**BIS chloride limit:** 250 mg/L acceptable, 1000 mg/L permissible

**Source-specific context:**
- Coastal AP (Nellore, Guntur coast): Saltwater intrusion very common
- Vijayawada shallow borewells: TDS-related saltiness
- Borewell near sea/estuary: High probability saltwater intrusion

**Advisory:** RO system for salt removal. Do not drink if very salty
(dehydrating at very high TDS). Contact CGWB for aquifer assessment.

---

### 3.3 Bitter Taste

**Primary contaminants:**
1. High sulphates (SO4) — above 400 mg/L
2. High TDS combined — above 1000 mg/L
3. Possible chemical contamination

**Advisory:** RO system. Get water tested for sulphates.

---

### 3.4 No Unusual Taste But Gastrointestinal Issues

**Primary concern:** Microbial contamination (coliform, E.coli)
**Confidence:** High (0.75) when multiple household members affected
**Risk:** HIGH

**Indicators of microbial cause:**
- Multiple family members affected at same time
- Symptoms appear 6–24 hours after water consumption
- Worse after rain (increased surface contamination)
- Hand pump or open well source

**Advisory:** BOIL ALL WATER immediately. If multiple people affected,
contact local health authority. Do not use water from same source
until tested and cleared. Use bottled water for drinking and cooking.

## 4. Visual Deposit and Staining Diagnosis

### 4.1 White Scale Inside Vessels and Kettles

**Primary cause:** High TDS / High calcium hardness
**Confidence:** Very High (0.92)
**BIS limit:** TDS 500 mg/L, Hardness 200 mg/L

**WaterSentinel interpretation:**
Thick white scale in kettle + vessel = TDS likely above 500 mg/L.
Very thick scale removing with difficulty = TDS possibly above 1000 mg/L.

**Health relevance:** Prolonged consumption above 500 mg/L TDS associated
with kidney stone formation, particularly calcium oxalate stones.

**Advisory:** RO system is the only effective solution. Boiling
concentrates TDS — does not remove it.

---

### 4.2 Orange or Yellow Staining on Pipes and Fixtures

**Primary cause:** Iron (Fe) — very high confidence (0.95)
**Advisory:** Iron removal filter. See section 2.1.

---

### 4.3 Black Staining on Fixtures

**Primary cause:** Manganese (Mn) — high confidence (0.80)
**Advisory:** Oxidation filter with manganese greensand. See section 2.2.

---

### 4.4 Black Particles in Water

**Primary causes:**
1. Manganese oxide particles — settled manganese
2. Degraded rubber washers in taps/pipes
3. Activated carbon from old filter cartridge (replace filter)

**Advisory:** If particles persist after changing tap washers and
replacing filter, test for manganese. Oxidation filter if confirmed.

## 5. Source-Specific High-Probability Combinations

These are the most common contamination profiles observed in Indian cities,
by water source type:

### 5.1 Borewell — Deccan Plateau (Hyderabad western areas)
Most likely: H2S + Iron (together, very common)
Common: High TDS
Less common: Fluoride trace amounts
Rare: Nitrate (unless near agriculture)

### 5.2 Borewell — Alluvial Plains (Vijayawada, Godavari delta)
Most likely: High TDS + High Hardness
Common: Iron (in low-lying flood plain areas)
Less common: Nitrate (near agriculture)
Rare: H2S (shallower aquifers, less anaerobic)

### 5.3 Municipal Pipeline — Hyderabad (HMWSSB)
Source water: Generally BIS-compliant at treatment plant
Post-treatment issues:
- Old pipeline: Iron from pipe corrosion
- Storage tank (not cleaned): Microbial contamination, H2S
- Pressure drop event: Sewage back-siphoning (rare but serious)

### 5.4 Hand Pump — Peri-Urban
Most likely: Iron (ferrous aquifer)
Common: Bacterial contamination (shared infrastructure, surface entry)
Post-monsoon: Elevated turbidity and microbial load

### 5.5 Open Well
Most likely: Bacterial contamination — assume always present
Common: Turbidity, algae (if exposed to sunlight)
Advisory: NEVER drink from open well without treatment.
Use only for non-drinking purposes.

## 6. Compound Symptom Diagnosis

When multiple symptoms are reported together:

| Symptom Combination | Most Likely Diagnosis | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| Egg smell + Yellow colour | H2S + Iron together | 0.90 |
| Yellow colour + Metallic taste | Iron contamination | 0.95 |
| White deposits + No smell | High TDS / Hardness | 0.88 |
| Black water + Faecal smell | Sewage contamination | 0.85 |
| Blue-green stain + Metallic taste | Copper corrosion | 0.88 |
| No symptom + Stomach issues | Microbial / Nitrate | 0.70 |
| Egg smell + Musty smell | H2S + Bacterial (dirty tank) | 0.75 |
| Yellow water + White deposits | Iron + High TDS | 0.85 |
| Salty taste + Coastal location | Saltwater intrusion | 0.90 |
