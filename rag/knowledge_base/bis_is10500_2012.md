# BIS IS 10500:2012 — Indian Standard: Drinking Water Specification
## Second Revision | Bureau of Indian Standards

This document contains the Indian drinking water quality standards as defined
by the Bureau of Indian Standards under IS 10500:2012. All WaterSentinel
diagnoses reference these standards when assessing water safety.

---

## 1. Scope

This standard specifies requirements for drinking water intended for human
consumption. It applies to all water sources including groundwater (borewells,
hand pumps, open wells) and surface water (municipal supply from rivers,
reservoirs, and treatment plants).

---

## 2. Physical Parameters

### 2.1 Colour
- Acceptable limit: 5 Hazen units
- Permissible limit (no alternative): 15 Hazen units
- Method: Visual comparison with standard colour discs
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** Yellow or brown colour indicates iron or
  manganese. Blue-green indicates copper. Black indicates manganese or
  sewage contamination.

### 2.2 Odour
- Acceptable: Unobjectionable (no specific limit)
- Test: Threshold Odour Number (TON) at 25°C
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** Egg smell = hydrogen sulphide (H2S).
  Musty smell = algae or organic matter. Chlorine smell = normal
  municipal treatment.

### 2.3 Taste
- Acceptable: Agreeable (no specific limit)
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** Metallic taste = iron or manganese.
  Salty taste = high TDS or chlorides. Bitter taste = sulphates or
  high TDS. Soapy taste = high pH or sodium.

### 2.4 Turbidity
- Acceptable limit: 1 NTU (Nephelometric Turbidity Unit)
- Permissible limit: 5 NTU
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** Milky water that does not clear = high
  turbidity, possible contamination. Milky water that clears after
  standing = dissolved air (harmless).

### 2.5 pH
- Acceptable range: 6.5 to 8.5
- No permissible relaxation
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** Very low pH (acidic) = corrosive water,
  leaches metals from pipes. Very high pH (alkaline) = soapy taste,
  may indicate chemical contamination.

## 3. Chemical Parameters

### 3.1 Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)
- Acceptable limit: 500 mg/L
- Permissible limit (no alternative): 2000 mg/L
- **Health impact:** High TDS causes calcification. Prolonged consumption
  of water with TDS above 500 mg/L is associated with kidney stone
  formation (nephrolithiasis), particularly calcium oxalate stones.
  High TDS also causes scale buildup in pipes and appliances.
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** White deposits on vessels and taps =
  high TDS / calcium hardness. This is one of the most common complaints
  in Indian cities, especially in borewell water.
- **Treatment:** Reverse Osmosis (RO) is the only effective home
  treatment for high TDS. Boiling does NOT reduce TDS.

### 3.2 Total Hardness (as CaCO3)
- Acceptable limit: 200 mg/L
- Permissible limit: 600 mg/L
- **Health impact:** Very hard water linked to kidney stones. Causes
  scale buildup. Makes soap less effective (requires more soap).
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** White scale inside kettles and vessels,
  white ring marks around taps = hard water.

### 3.3 Iron (Fe)
- Acceptable limit: 0.3 mg/L
- No permissible relaxation specified
- **Health impact:** Iron above 0.3 mg/L causes yellow to brown
  discolouration. Metallic taste. Stains laundry and plumbing yellow
  or orange. Not immediately toxic but indicates other contamination
  risk. High iron water supports bacterial growth (iron bacteria).
- **Source:** Common in borewells drilled into iron-rich aquifers,
  particularly in Deccan Plateau geology (Hyderabad, Pune) and
  alluvial plains (Godavari delta in AP).
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** Yellow or brown water, orange pipe
  staining, metallic taste = iron contamination. Very high confidence
  indicator.
- **Treatment:** Iron removal filter using greensand or birm media.
  UV filter does NOT remove iron. RO removes iron but wastes water.

### 3.4 Manganese (Mn)
- Acceptable limit: 0.1 mg/L
- Permissible limit: 0.3 mg/L
- **Health impact:** Manganese above limits causes neurological effects
  with long-term exposure. Black or dark brown discolouration.
  Black particles in water.
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** Black water or dark brown water with
  no sewage smell = manganese. Black particles in water = manganese
  oxide precipitate.
- **Treatment:** Oxidation filter with manganese greensand media.

### 3.5 Fluoride (F)
- Acceptable limit: 1.0 mg/L
- Permissible limit: 1.5 mg/L
- **Health impact:** Fluoride below 0.5 mg/L causes dental problems.
  Fluoride above 1.5 mg/L causes dental fluorosis (mottled teeth).
  Fluoride above 4.0 mg/L causes skeletal fluorosis (joint pain,
  bone deformities). India has one of the highest rates of endemic
  fluorosis globally.
- **High-risk areas in India:** Nalgonda (Telangana) — historically
  one of the worst affected districts globally. Prakasam, Kurnool,
  Nellore (Andhra Pradesh). Rajasthan, Gujarat, Haryana.
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** No visible symptom (fluoride is
  odourless and colourless). Joint pain, dental mottling, bone pain
  = possible fluoride exposure. Location in Nalgonda = high
  probability of fluoride issue.
- **Treatment:** RO is the only effective home treatment for fluoride.
  Activated alumina filter is an alternative. Boiling does NOT reduce
  fluoride — it concentrates it.

### 3.6 Nitrate (NO3)
- Acceptable limit: 45 mg/L
- No permissible relaxation
- **Health impact:** High nitrate causes methaemoglobinaemia (blue
  baby syndrome) in infants under 6 months. Indicates agricultural
  runoff, sewage contamination, or septic tank leakage.
- **Source:** Borewells near agricultural land, shallow borewells
  near septic tanks or open drains.
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** No visible symptom. Source is borewell
  near agricultural land = elevated nitrate risk. Infants in household
  = critical to test.
- **Treatment:** RO or ion exchange. Boiling concentrates nitrates.

### 3.7 Sulphate (SO4)
- Acceptable limit: 200 mg/L
- Permissible limit: 400 mg/L
- **Health impact:** Above 400 mg/L causes laxative effect, diarrhoea.
  Contributes to bitter taste.
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** Bitter taste + sulphur smell may indicate
  both sulphate and H2S presence.

### 3.8 Hydrogen Sulphide (H2S)
- Acceptable limit: 0.05 mg/L
- No permissible relaxation
- **Health impact:** Odour detectable at 0.0005 mg/L (well below
  health threshold). At concentrations found in groundwater, H2S
  is typically safe for bathing and washing. Unsafe for drinking
  above 0.05 mg/L. At very high concentrations (industrial levels
  not found in domestic water) can be toxic.
- **Source:** Anaerobic bacteria in deep borewells, especially in
  Deccan Plateau geology. Common in borewells deeper than 200 feet.
  Also from sulphur-reducing bacteria in unclean storage tanks.
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** Egg smell or rotten egg smell = H2S.
  VERY high confidence indicator. Safe to use for bathing even with
  this smell. Do NOT drink without treatment.
- **Treatment:** Aeration (running water through air to release H2S gas)
  + chlorination. Activated carbon filter. NOT removed by UV or RO alone.

### 3.9 Copper (Cu)
- Acceptable limit: 0.05 mg/L
- Permissible limit: 1.5 mg/L
- **Health impact:** Above permissible limit causes liver damage and
  gastrointestinal distress. Blue-green staining is the key indicator.
- **Source:** Corrosion of copper pipes, especially in acidic water
  (pH below 7.0).
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** Blue-green staining on brass taps,
  ceramic sinks, or white vessels = copper leaching from pipes.
- **Treatment:** Raise pH of water (reduce acidity). Replace copper
  pipes if severely corroded. RO removes copper.

### 3.10 Chloride (Cl)
- Acceptable limit: 250 mg/L
- Permissible limit: 1000 mg/L
- **Health impact:** Above 250 mg/L causes salty taste. High chlorides
  in coastal areas indicate saltwater intrusion into aquifers.
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** Salty taste in coastal cities (Vijayawada,
  coastal AP) = possible saltwater intrusion, especially in borewells
  less than 100 feet deep near sea level.

### 3.11 Arsenic (As)
- Acceptable limit: 0.01 mg/L
- No permissible relaxation
- **Health impact:** Carcinogenic. Long-term exposure causes skin
  lesions, cancers. No visible symptom in water.
- **High-risk areas:** West Bengal, Assam, Bihar (Ganga plains).
  Lower risk in peninsular India (Deccan Plateau) but not zero.
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** No visible symptom. Recommend lab test
  if in high-risk area.

## 4. Bacteriological Parameters

### 4.1 Total Coliform Organisms
- Acceptable limit: 0 MPN/100 mL (most probable number)
- No permissible relaxation for drinking water
- **Health impact:** Indicates faecal contamination. Presence of
  coliforms = high risk of waterborne diseases including typhoid,
  cholera, dysentery, hepatitis A.
- **Source:** Sewage contamination, septic tank leakage, open well
  exposure, dirty storage tanks, cross-connection between sewage
  and water pipes.
- **WaterSentinel relevance:** No visible symptom in many cases. Black
  water = possible sewage contamination (very high coliform risk).
  Stomach issues in multiple household members = possible coliform.
  Open well or hand pump = always assume coliform risk without treatment.
- **Treatment:** UV purifier (kills bacteria), chlorination, boiling
  (100% effective if water boiled vigorously for 1 minute).

### 4.2 E. coli / Thermotolerant Coliforms
- Acceptable limit: 0 MPN/100 mL
- No permissible relaxation
- Direct indicator of recent faecal contamination.

## 5. Key Takeaways for WaterSentinel Diagnosis

| Symptom | Most Likely Contaminant | BIS Limit | Safe to Drink? | Safe to Bathe? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Egg smell | H2S | 0.05 mg/L | No (treat first) | Yes |
| Yellow/brown water | Iron (Fe) | 0.3 mg/L | No (treat first) | Yes |
| Black water | Manganese / Sewage | 0.1 mg/L Mn | No | Caution |
| White deposits | High TDS / Hardness | 500 mg/L TDS | Caution | Yes |
| Blue-green stain | Copper (Cu) | 0.05 mg/L | Caution | Yes |
| Metallic taste | Iron / Manganese | See above | No (treat) | Yes |
| Salty taste | High TDS / Chlorides | 500/250 mg/L | Caution | Yes |
| No symptom + stomach issues | Coliform / Nitrate | 0 MPN/45 mg/L | No | Yes |
| No symptom (Nalgonda area) | Fluoride | 1.0 mg/L | Get tested | Yes |
